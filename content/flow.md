# **What is Flow?** #
{: style="text-align: center"}

Flow, colloquially known as "the zone", is a mental state in which one is completely immersed in a given activity -- all other worries and occupations flake away to reveal the "optimal experience".

During flow, sensory input not related to the task at hand is filtered out, and an individual may go many hours at a time without eating, taking a break, or even taking notice of bodily discomfort. If this occurs to the point where it poses a threat to the individual, it is often referred to as *hyperfocus*.

As per revealed in an [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/s/415041/new-measure-of-human-brain-processing-speed/) column, the brain processes information at around **60 bits per second**, depending on the activity. Thus, with an activity demanding enough, or worthy of one’s full attention, complete immersion on this level becomes a possibility.

---

According to Mihaly, the key to living a happy, fulfilled life is to live with the ability to induce flow regularly, in a variety of occupations.
> "People who are self-actualized tend to achieve flow often."
> -- <cite>Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi</cite>

Those who are capable of this feat he terms *autotelic individuals*. The term *autotelic*, from the English "*auto-*" meaning self and the Greek "*telos*" meaning end, refers to the property of something being entirely self-contained. In the context of flow, this means for an activity to be intrinsically rewarding – for an activity to be a goal in itself, not necessarily being part of a larger plan of action.

---

The hardest part of achieving flow may be the requirement of having autonomy in its execution. Flow must be achieved subconsciously, and this often requires the individual in question to have at least a basic mastery of the process at hand. Furthermore, an activity must give immediate feedback, be it through observation of short term goals, or by comparison to a set of pre-internalized standards. These criteria closely mirror the criteria for a passion, enumerated on the previous page.

A further difficulty in achieving flow is the balancing of level of skill and challenge. When skill exceeds challenge, a task becomes boring and does not elicit complete focus. When challenge exceeds skill, the task can become frustration and can induce anxiety. A task with a steep learning curve can seem overwhelming, but if tackled at the right rate can be the perfect catalyst for flow.

All things enacted correctly, and one soon finds themselves in an environment separate from everyday reality. And from testimonial statements, "one is happy -- at least as happy as is possible without being aware of it".

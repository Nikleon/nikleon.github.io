# **My Personal Experience With Flow** #
{: style="text-align: center"}

As a certain, modern age scientific powerhouse once said:
> “Strenuous intellectual work and looking at God’s nature are the reconciling, fortifying, yet relentlessly strict angels that will lead me through all of life’s troubles.”
> -- <cite>Albert Einstein (18 y/o)</cite>

Indeed, when one becomes sufficiently devoted to their craft, all other concerns seem to fall perfectly into place.

---

In my own experience, whilst I spend the majority of my time reading and coding and exploiting every possible way I can put off work for a class, the workload and intensity of said classes get consistently less demanding. Grades become easier to sustain, and the content of courses more and more frequently overlap with subjects I have already exhausted independently.

Outside of school, recognition and opportunity come as a side-effect of my passion for studying. I have a tendency to get in touch with those who expresses a similar devotion to their craft, especially those who’s occupations fall within the natural sciences. Over time, my connections have increased multifold, and so have the opportunities available to me.

Most importantly though, I am fully content with my approach, proud of my progress, and confident in my future. This is the happiness that flow has provided me, and while my goals remain rigid and lofty, it will continue to be so.

My own experience with flow can be summed up as having been “in constant pursuit of knowledge” – a cliché motivation, but a valid one nevertheless. As long as I am learning, not for the sake of achieving a grade, but for the sake of learning itself, I can enter flow on demand, and this is quality is the basis for my entire lifestyle.

# **Suffering is Happiness** #
{: style="text-align: center"}

![David Brooks](/assets/David_Brooks.jpeg)
{: style="text-align: center"}
_David Brooks, NY Times author_
{: style="text-align: center"}

Now I am aware the title sounds masochistic, so I'll rephrase it in the words of New York Times opinion editor, David Brooks:
> "People who seek this proper rejoinder to ordeal sense that they are at a deeper level than the level of happiness and individual utility."
> -- <cite>David Brooks</cite>

What Brooks intends to convey is that -- once a person goes through suffering, they come out forever changed. And this change can be key to your future prospects of happiness, as it has the power to completely re-establish your worldview and meta-emotions.

So when you are truly suffering, try to take reign of your re-adjustment and direct it towards something worth living for. I'd recommend choosing a goal like mine, which provides plenty of opportunity for flow.
